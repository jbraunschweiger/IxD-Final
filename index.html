Oasis Case Study

IxD Final Project


Overview

Context

In their third year, Washington University in St. Louis medical students are required to complete different clerkships: neurology, psychiatry, pediatrics, Ob/GYN, surgery and medicine. One requirement for these real-world experiences is in the form of a “requirement log,” which is meant to ensure that each student is meeting the necessary criteria in each clerkships. The tool used to record these logs is Oasis, an online educational program that allows admistration to review the students progress.

Problem Statements

How can we ehance the Oasis requirement log system to improve the user’s eduational/ clerkship experience?

How might we create a digital experience that encourages easy and reliable data entry for very busy third year medical students? (class question outlined in assignment)

Users and Audince

We designed for Washington University in St. Louis third year medical students (and administration), here's an example persona for our target audience:

Name: Riley

Backstory: 3rd year medical student at WashU

Scenario: He’s almost done with his clerkship rotation and needs to record his patient interactions to ensure he fulfilled his requirements.

Emotions: Stressed, annoyed, unmotivated by requirement log (motivated by patients)

Motivations:

  • Direct: Fulfill requirement, pass the clerkship + medical school, keeps you on track (feel organized)
  • Indirect: Wants to learn how to be a better doctor, wants to keep track of patient interactions

Behiavors:

  • Take a mixture of handwritten notes and typed notes on phone apps
  • Follow up with patients through Epic
  • Usually done on computer, occasionally on phone
  • Try to do it quickly/get it over with
  • Procrastinates on filling out logs

Ability Inhibitors:

  • Time
  • System Errors


Scope

Project Scope

The objective of this project was to redesign OASIS, a program used by third-year medical students to input patient logs from clinical rotations. The project was tasked to us by our partners from the medical school at WashU and we gleaned insights from our partners and third-year medical students in order to construct our solution. Our solution to the project goal was to create a mobile app with a primary focus on streamlining the process of inputting patient data and making requirement checklists easily accessible and understandable.

Constraints

  • Due to the pandemic, this course was fully remote (online only), which meant we were not able to tour or observe Washu medical facilites.
  • Due to HIPAA regulations, we were not allowed direct access to the Oasis platform. Instead we were given brief presentations on the primary functions, which meant we only had a basic understanding of program.

Interviews

Our lack of knowledge in the operations of medical school meant we needed to establish a basic understanding of the third year clerkship program. We were able to hear presentations from three WashU faculty and staff, in addition to interviewing a current student. Since each group in the class was assigned a different student to interview, we also had access to these other interviews.
